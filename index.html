About Heliospheric Current Sheets

Heliospheric Current Sheets, also known as HCS, is loosely defined is a thin layer of plasma that divides two oppositely directed global heliospheric magnetic fields originating from the sun. It is believed that the HCS is generated at the cusp of the streamer belt and is the interplanetary extension of the solar neutral line. The Heliospheric Current Sheet is the largest and persistent solar wind structure that permeates the entire solar system. The HCS can be described as having a warped shape after streching out of the sun by solar wind, it is also surrounded by a region of plasma called "The Heliospheric Plasma Sheet(HPS)". HCS can be characterized by having a local maximum in the proton density and a local minimum in proton temperature and speed.
